About Muscat

Muscat, the capital city of Oman, is a coastal city where tradition and modern life coexist in harmony. Nestled between the rugged Al Hajar Mountains and the Arabian Sea, Muscat offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and contemporary development.

Known for its whitewashed buildings, grand mosques, and historic forts, Muscat has preserved its architectural identity while embracing modernization. Unlike many global capitals, the city maintains a low-rise skyline, reflecting Oman’s commitment to cultural continuity and urban balance.

Muscat has long been an important maritime hub, connecting Oman to East Africa, India, and beyond. This rich seafaring history has shaped the city’s diverse culture, cuisine, and welcoming spirit. Visitors can explore traditional souqs, scenic corniches, and world-class cultural landmarks.

Whether you are exploring Mutrah’s historic waterfront, hiking nearby wadis, or enjoying the calm beaches along the coast, Muscat offers an experience that is authentic, peaceful, and deeply connected to Oman’s heritage.
